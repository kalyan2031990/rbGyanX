1. What is rbGyanX_basic?

⚠️ IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER:
This tool provides decision support and should not replace clinical judgment. All results should be reviewed by qualified medical physicists and radiation oncologists.
This software is intended for research and educational purposes. Clinical decisions should be made by qualified medical professionals.

rbGyanX_basic is a radiobiology-guided clinical decision support framework designed for medical physicists, radiation oncologists, and clinical researchers. It provides comprehensive tools for:

  • DVH Preprocessing: Intelligent parsing and canonicalization of Dose-Volume Histogram files from multiple treatment planning systems
  • Physical Dose Metrics: Automated calculation of standard dose metrics for both target structures (PTV/GTV/CTV) and organs at risk (OARs)
  • Radiobiological Modeling: Traditional models (LKB, RS, Poisson) and machine learning models (ANN, XGBoost) for TCP and NTCP prediction
  • Clinical Factors Analysis: Exploration of relationships between clinical variables and treatment outcomes
  • Quality Assurance: Automated checks for model reliability, overfitting detection, and data quality
  • Therapeutic Ratio Analysis: Integration of TCP and NTCP for comprehensive treatment plan evaluation
Scope: rbGyanX_basic focuses on retrospective analysis and research applications. It does not provide real-time clinical decision support or integrate with treatment planning systems.

5.3 Novel Features (rbGyanX CDSS Framework)

rbGyanX includes advanced radiobiological features that extend beyond standard TCP/NTCP calculators:

FDVH (Fractionation-Aware DVH)

What it does: Converts physical DVH to biologically effective dose (BED)-normalized DVH
Why use it: Enables hypofractionation modeling (SBRT/SRS), cross-fractionation scheme comparison, and re-irradiation assessment
When to use: When analyzing non-standard fractionation schemes or comparing different fractionation protocols
Mathematical basis: BED = n · d · (1 + d/(α/β)) where n = fractions, d = dose per fraction, α/β = tissue parameter
Output: BED-normalized DVH with columns labeled "BED-DVH" and "DVH_Type = FDVH"

uTCP (Uncertainty-Aware TCP)

What it does: Propagates parameter uncertainty through TCP models to provide confidence bounds
Why use it: Reports TCP = 0.78 ± 0.11 instead of false precision, enabling honest uncertainty quantification
When to use: When you need to understand prediction reliability and parameter sensitivity
Mathematical basis: Monte Carlo sampling of parameter distributions to estimate prediction variance
Output: Additional columns: TCP_mean, TCP_std, TCP_95CI_low, TCP_95CI_high
Note: uTCP reflects parameter uncertainty, not clinical outcome variance

TWI (Therapeutic Window Index)

What it does: Intelligent plan comparison with organ-specific risk weighting
Why use it: Provides a single metric that balances tumor control (TCP) against weighted normal tissue risk (NTCP)
When to use: When comparing multiple treatment plans or evaluating plan quality
Mathematical basis: TWI = TCP - Σ(λ_k · NTCP_k) where λ_k = organ-specific risk weight
Risk weights: Spinal Cord (1.0), Brainstem (1.0), Lung (0.8), Heart (0.9), Pharynx (0.5), Parotid (0.3)
Output: therapeutic_ratios.xlsx with TWI column and TWI_Interpretation (Favorable/Moderate/Unfavorable)
Limitation: Decision support only – no automated clinical decisions

CCS (Cohort Consistency Score - ML Safety Gate)

What it does: Prevents unsafe ML extrapolation by detecting out-of-distribution (OOD) patients
Why use it: ML models can be overconfident on patients who differ from training data. CCS prevents this.
When to use: When using ML models, especially with institutional or external training data
Mathematical basis: CCS = exp(-0.5 · Mahalanobis²) where Mahalanobis distance measures deviation from training cohort
Safety gate: If CCS < threshold (default 0.1), ML predictions are disabled and traditional models are used
Output: CCS value in ML output table, warnings when patients are OOD
Action: ML prediction suppressed due to cohort mismatch → use traditional models only

  • No treatment optimization
  • No autonomous decisions
  • No outcome prediction guarantee
  • No real-time clinical decision support
ML Models Enabled: ANN, XGBoost
Description: Machine learning models for enhanced prediction accuracy
SHAP Explainability: Enabled for model interpretability
